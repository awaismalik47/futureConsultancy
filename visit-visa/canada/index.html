Canada Visitor Visa Requirements

Requirements for temporary residence as a visitor to Canada. Prepare your application with clear, up-to-date guidance.

Home / Visit Visa / Canada
Overview Eligibility Required Documents Application Process Processing & Fee Important Tips

Overview

A Canada visitor (tourist) visa allows you to enter Canada for tourism, family visits, or business. Depending on your nationality, you may need a visa or an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA). Applications are submitted online; biometrics are required for most applicants. Proof of funds and ties to your home country are critical.

Eligibility

  • Purpose: Tourism, family visit, business (short trips), or other temporary stay.
  • Intent: Must leave Canada at the end of your authorized stay.
  • Funds: Sufficient funds to cover your stay and return travel.
  • Passport: Valid for the duration of your stay; some nationalities need 6+ months.
  • Health & character: May need medical exam or police certificate in some cases.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport and copies of previous visas/travel.
  • Completed application form (online) and supporting documents as per checklist.
  • Passport-size photographs meeting IRCC specifications.
  • Proof of financial support (bank statements, employment letter, sponsorship).
  • Purpose of visit (invitation letter, itinerary, hotel bookings).
  • Proof of ties to home country (job, property, family).
  • Biometrics (fingerprints and photo) at a Visa Application Centre (VAC) if required.

Application Process

  1. Check if you need a visa or an eTA on the official IRCC website.
  2. Create an online account (IRCC portal) and complete the visitor visa application.
  3. Upload all required documents and pay the processing fee and biometrics fee (if applicable).
  4. Give your biometrics at a designated VAC when instructed.
  5. Wait for a decision; you may be asked for additional documents or an interview.
  6. If approved, you will receive a visa counterfoil in your passport or an eTA linked to your passport.

Important Tips

  • Apply well in advance; processing can take longer during peak seasons.
  • Provide clear proof of funds and strong ties to your home country.
  • Ensure all forms and supporting documents are complete and consistent.
  • If invited by family, include a notarized invitation and host’s status in Canada.
