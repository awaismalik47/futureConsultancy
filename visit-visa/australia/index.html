Australia Visitor Visa (Subclass 600) Requirements

Requirements for visiting Australia for tourism or business. Prepare a strong application with clear, professional guidance.

Home / Visit Visa / Australia
Overview Eligibility Required Documents Application Process Processing & Fee Important Tips

Overview

The Visitor visa (subclass 600) allows you to visit Australia for tourism, business visitor activities, or to visit family. Applications are lodged online via ImmiAccount. You must meet health and character requirements and show you have genuine intent to visit and will comply with visa conditions.

Eligibility

  • Purpose: Tourism, business visitor (e.g. meetings, conferences), or visiting family.
  • Intent: Genuine temporary entrant; must leave before visa expires.
  • Funds: Sufficient money to support yourself and cover your stay and travel.
  • Health: May need medical examination or chest X-ray depending on stay and origin.
  • Character: Must meet character requirements; police certificates may be required.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (with at least 6 months validity and blank pages).
  • Completed application form and supporting documents as per the document checklist.
  • Passport-size photograph meeting Australian visa photo requirements.
  • Proof of financial capacity (bank statements, employment letter, tax returns).
  • Evidence of employment or business and ties to home country.
  • Travel itinerary, accommodation, and purpose of visit.
  • Health insurance may be required for some streams or recommended for long stays.

Application Process

  1. Choose the correct stream (tourist, business visitor, sponsored family, etc.) on the Home Affairs website.
  2. Create an ImmiAccount and complete the online application; attach all required documents.
  3. Pay the visa application charge (VAC); biometrics may be required at a designated centre.
  4. Wait for assessment; the department may request more information or a health examination.
  5. If granted, you will receive a visa grant letter; check conditions and validity dates.
  6. No visa label is placed in passport; use the grant letter and VEVO for status.

Fees and processing times are set by the Australian Department of Home Affairs. Always verify on the official website.

Important Tips

  • Provide strong evidence of genuine temporary stay and ties to your home country.
  • Ensure financial documents show a clear, credible source of funds.
  • Declare all previous travel and visa history; inconsistencies can lead to refusal.
  • Apply in advance; avoid making travel commitments before visa grant.
